Fan Types Guide

Centrifugal Fans

Applications: General HVAC, high static pressure systems

Characteristics:

  • Backward inclined: High efficiency, clean air
  • Forward curved: Low velocity, space-constrained
  • Airfoil: Highest efficiency, quiet operation
  • Static Pressure: 0.5" - 10" wg

Best For: Supply air systems, built-up air handlers

Inline Centrifugal Fans

Applications: Duct-mounted systems, space-saving installations

Characteristics:

  • Compact design for inline mounting
  • Good efficiency at moderate pressures
  • Easy installation and maintenance
  • Static Pressure: 0.25" - 3.5" wg

Best For: Exhaust systems, bathroom/kitchen fans

Axial Fans

Applications: Low pressure, high volume air movement

Characteristics:

  • Propeller or tube-axial design
  • High airflow at low static pressure
  • Simple construction, low cost
  • Static Pressure: 0" - 1" wg

Best For: Ventilation, cooling towers, condensers

Mixed Flow Fans

Applications: Medium pressure applications

Characteristics:

  • Combines centrifugal and axial characteristics
  • Compact design with good efficiency
  • Moderate pressure capability
  • Static Pressure: 0.5" - 2.5" wg

Best For: Commercial ventilation, inline applications

Airflow (CFM)

Calculate required CFM based on room volume, air changes per hour (ACH), or cooling/heating loads. Add 10-15% safety factor for duct losses and future expansion.

Static Pressure

Account for all system resistances: filters, ductwork, fittings, grilles, and dampers. Typical values: 0.2-0.4" per 100 ft of duct, 0.08" per fitting.

Sound Levels

Consider noise criteria (NC) ratings for the space. Libraries and offices require NC-25 to NC-35. Use sound attenuators for noise-sensitive areas.

Energy Efficiency

Higher efficiency fans reduce operating costs. Look for AMCA-certified fans. Backward inclined and airfoil wheels offer best efficiency (75-85%).

Space Constraints

Inline fans save space but may be limited in pressure. Cabinet fans require fan rooms. Rooftop fans save indoor space but need weatherproofing.

Maintenance Access

Ensure adequate clearance for belt changes, bearing lubrication, and wheel cleaning. Belt-driven fans require more maintenance than direct-driven.
