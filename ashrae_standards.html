ASHRAE Standards

Key ASHRAE standards and guidelines for HVAC design and building mechanical systems. Essential reference for mechanical engineers and HVAC designers.

About ASHRAE

The American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE) is a global professional society that develops standards, guidelines, and best practices for HVAC systems and building design. These standards are widely adopted in building codes and industry practice worldwide.

Core ASHRAE Standards

üìã ASHRAE 90.1 - Energy Standard for Buildings

Purpose: Establishes minimum energy efficiency requirements for buildings except low-rise residential buildings.

Key Areas:

  • Building envelope requirements (walls, roofs, windows)
  • HVAC system efficiency requirements
  • Lighting power density limits
  • Service water heating efficiency
  • Prescriptive and performance-based compliance paths

Application: Required by many state and local building codes. Often referenced in green building certifications like LEED.

üí® ASHRAE 62.1 - Ventilation for Acceptable Indoor Air Quality

Purpose: Specifies minimum ventilation rates and indoor air quality requirements for commercial and institutional buildings.

Key Requirements:

  • Minimum outdoor air ventilation rates per person and per area
  • Ventilation effectiveness requirements
  • Air filtration and cleaning standards
  • Indoor air quality procedures
  • Natural ventilation design criteria

Application: Critical for HVAC system design and indoor air quality compliance.

üå°Ô∏è ASHRAE 55 - Thermal Environmental Conditions for Human Occupancy

Purpose: Defines thermal comfort conditions for building occupants.

Key Concepts:

  • Predicted Mean Vote (PMV) and Predicted Percentage Dissatisfied (PPD)
  • Acceptable temperature and humidity ranges
  • Air speed and radiant temperature considerations
  • Adaptive comfort model for naturally ventilated buildings
  • Vertical temperature gradients and asymmetric radiation

Application: Used to design HVAC systems that provide acceptable thermal comfort.

üè† ASHRAE 62.2 - Ventilation for Low-Rise Residential Buildings

Purpose: Provides minimum ventilation requirements for single-family homes and low-rise multi-family buildings.

Key Requirements:

  • Whole-house ventilation rates based on floor area and bedrooms
  • Local exhaust requirements for kitchens and bathrooms
  • Air filtration recommendations
  • Envelope air leakage limits
  • Mechanical ventilation system design

Application: Essential for residential HVAC system design and building code compliance.

üå± ASHRAE 189.1 - Standard for High-Performance Green Buildings

Purpose: Provides requirements for the design of high-performance green buildings.

Coverage Areas:

  • Site sustainability and exterior design
  • Water use efficiency
  • Energy efficiency (more stringent than 90.1)
  • Indoor environmental quality
  • Construction and operations plans

Application: Referenced in green building programs and sustainable design projects.
