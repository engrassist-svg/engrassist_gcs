Airflow Calculations

Volumetric Flow Rate

Q = A × V

Where:

  • Q = Volumetric flow rate (CFM or ft³/min)
  • A = Cross-sectional area (ft²)
  • V = Velocity (ft/min or FPM)

Example:

Calculate airflow through a 24" × 18" duct with air velocity of 1200 FPM:

  • Area = (24/12) × (18/12) = 2 ft × 1.5 ft = 3 ft²
  • Q = 3 ft² × 1200 FPM = 3,600 CFM

Air Changes Per Hour (ACH)

ACH = (CFM × 60) / Room Volume

Where:

  • CFM = Airflow rate (cubic feet per minute)
  • 60 = Minutes per hour
  • Room Volume = Length × Width × Height (ft³)

Example:

Calculate ACH for a 20' × 15' × 10' room with 500 CFM supply:

  • Volume = 20 × 15 × 10 = 3,000 ft³
  • ACH = (500 × 60) / 3,000 = 10 air changes per hour

Mass Flow Rate

ṁ = ρ × Q

Where:

  • ṁ = Mass flow rate (lb/min)
  • ρ = Air density (lb/ft³, typically 0.075 lb/ft³ at standard conditions)
  • Q = Volumetric flow rate (CFM)

Example:

Calculate mass flow for 5,000 CFM at standard conditions:

  • ṁ = 0.075 lb/ft³ × 5,000 CFM = 375 lb/min

Heat Transfer Calculations

Sensible Heat Transfer

Qs = 1.08 × CFM × ΔT

Where:

  • Qs = Sensible heat (BTU/hr)
  • 1.08 = Constant (0.24 BTU/lb·°F × 60 min/hr × 0.075 lb/ft³)
  • CFM = Airflow rate (ft³/min)
  • ΔT = Temperature difference (°F)

Example:

Calculate sensible cooling for 2,000 CFM with 20°F temperature drop:

  • Qs = 1.08 × 2,000 × 20 = 43,200 BTU/hr = 3.6 tons

Latent Heat Transfer

Ql = 0.68 × CFM × Δω

Where:

  • Ql = Latent heat (BTU/hr)
  • 0.68 = Constant (1,061 BTU/lb × 60 min/hr × 0.075 lb/ft³ / 7,000 grains/lb)
  • CFM = Airflow rate (ft³/min)
  • Δω = Humidity ratio difference (grains/lb)

Example:

Calculate latent cooling for 2,000 CFM removing 30 grains/lb moisture:

  • Ql = 0.68 × 2,000 × 30 = 40,800 BTU/hr = 3.4 tons

Total Heat Transfer

Qt = 4.5 × CFM × Δh

Where:

  • Qt = Total heat (BTU/hr)
  • 4.5 = Constant (60 min/hr × 0.075 lb/ft³)
  • CFM = Airflow rate (ft³/min)
  • Δh = Enthalpy difference (BTU/lb)

Example:

Calculate total cooling for 3,000 CFM with 8 BTU/lb enthalpy change:

  • Qt = 4.5 × 3,000 × 8 = 108,000 BTU/hr = 9 tons

Conduction Heat Transfer

Q = U × A × ΔT

Where:

  • Q = Heat transfer rate (BTU/hr)
  • U = Overall heat transfer coefficient (BTU/hr·ft²·°F)
  • A = Surface area (ft²)
  • ΔT = Temperature difference (°F)

Example:

Calculate heat loss through 500 ft² wall with U=0.08, ΔT=40°F:

  • Q = 0.08 × 500 × 40 = 1,600 BTU/hr

Psychrometric Calculations

Relative Humidity from Wet Bulb & Dry Bulb

RH ≈ 100 × (Pws,wb - (Patm × (Tdb - Twb) / 1800)) / Pws,db

Where:

  • RH = Relative humidity (%)
  • Tdb = Dry bulb temperature (°F)
  • Twb = Wet bulb temperature (°F)
  • Pws = Saturation vapor pressure
  • Patm = Atmospheric pressure (psia)

Note: For precise calculations, use psychrometric charts or tables. This approximation is valid for typical HVAC conditions.

Humidity Ratio

ω = 0.622 × (Pv / (Patm - Pv))

Where:

  • ω = Humidity ratio (lb water/lb dry air)
  • 0.622 = Ratio of molecular weights (water/air)
  • Pv = Partial pressure of water vapor (psia)
  • Patm = Atmospheric pressure (psia, typically 14.696 at sea level)

Conversion: To convert to grains/lb, multiply by 7,000 (grains per pound)

Dew Point Temperature

Tdp = Tdb - ((100 - RH) / 5)

Where:

  • Tdp = Dew point temperature (°F, approximate)
  • Tdb = Dry bulb temperature (°F)
  • RH = Relative humidity (%)

Note: This is a simplified approximation. For precise calculations, use psychrometric charts or the Magnus-Tetens formula.

Enthalpy of Moist Air

h = 0.24 × Tdb + ω × (1,061 + 0.444 × Tdb)

Where:

  • h = Enthalpy (BTU/lb dry air)
  • 0.24 = Specific heat of dry air (BTU/lb·°F)
  • Tdb = Dry bulb temperature (°F)
  • ω = Humidity ratio (lb water/lb dry air)
  • 1,061 = Latent heat of vaporization at 32°F (BTU/lb)
  • 0.444 = Specific heat of water vapor (BTU/lb·°F)

Fluid Mechanics Calculations

Water Flow Rate

Q = V × A

Where:

  • Q = Flow rate (GPM)
  • V = Velocity (ft/s)
  • A = Cross-sectional area (ft²)
  • Conversion: GPM = (ft³/s) × 448.83

Example:

Calculate flow in 4" pipe (ID = 4.026") at 6 ft/s velocity:

  • Area = π × (4.026/12)² / 4 = 0.0884 ft²
  • Q = 6 × 0.0884 × 448.83 = 238 GPM

Pressure Drop in Pipes (Darcy-Weisbach)

ΔP = f × (L/D) × (ρV²/2)

Where:

  • ΔP = Pressure drop (psf)
  • f = Friction factor (dimensionless)
  • L = Pipe length (ft)
  • D = Pipe diameter (ft)
  • ρ = Fluid density (lb/ft³)
  • V = Velocity (ft/s)

Simplified for water: ΔP (ft of head) = f × (L/D) × (V²/2g), where g = 32.2 ft/s²

Reynolds Number

Re = ρVD / μ = VD / ν

Where:

  • Re = Reynolds number (dimensionless)
  • ρ = Density (lb/ft³)
  • V = Velocity (ft/s)
  • D = Diameter (ft)
  • μ = Dynamic viscosity (lb/ft·s)
  • ν = Kinematic viscosity (ft²/s)

Flow Regimes:

  • Laminar: Re < 2,300
  • Transitional: 2,300 < Re < 4,000
  • Turbulent: Re > 4,000

Bernoulli's Equation

P₁/ρ + V₁²/2 + gz₁ = P₂/ρ + V₂²/2 + gz₂

Where:

  • P = Pressure (psf)
  • ρ = Density (lb/ft³)
  • V = Velocity (ft/s)
  • g = Gravitational acceleration (32.2 ft/s²)
  • z = Elevation (ft)

Note: Assumes incompressible, inviscid flow along a streamline.

Thermodynamic Calculations

Coefficient of Performance (COP) - Cooling

COPcooling = Qc / W

Where:

  • COP = Coefficient of performance (dimensionless)
  • Qc = Cooling capacity (BTU/hr)
  • W = Power input (BTU/hr)
  • Note: 1 kW = 3,412 BTU/hr

Example:

Calculate COP for 120,000 BTU/hr chiller using 12 kW:

  • W = 12 kW × 3,412 = 40,944 BTU/hr
  • COP = 120,000 / 40,944 = 2.93

Energy Efficiency Ratio (EER)

EER = Qc (BTU/hr) / W (watts)

Where:

  • EER = Energy efficiency ratio (BTU/hr per watt)
  • Qc = Cooling capacity (BTU/hr)
  • W = Power input (watts)

Relationship to COP: EER = COP × 3.412

Carnot Efficiency

ηCarnot = 1 - (TL / TH)

Where:

  • η = Efficiency (dimensionless)
  • TL = Low temperature reservoir (°R = °F + 459.67)
  • TH = High temperature reservoir (°R)

Note: Carnot efficiency represents the theoretical maximum efficiency. Real systems are always less efficient.

Specific Heat Capacity

Q = m × cp × ΔT

Where:

  • Q = Heat transfer (BTU)
  • m = Mass (lb)
  • cp = Specific heat (BTU/lb·°F)
  • ΔT = Temperature change (°F)

Common values:

  • Water: cp = 1.0 BTU/lb·°F
  • Air: cp = 0.24 BTU/lb·°F
  • Glycol (50%): cp ≈ 0.85 BTU/lb·°F

Load Calculation Formulas

Cooling Load from Occupants

Q = N × (Qs + Ql)

Where:

  • Q = Total heat from occupants (BTU/hr)
  • N = Number of occupants
  • Qs = Sensible heat per person (BTU/hr)
  • Ql = Latent heat per person (BTU/hr)

Typical values at 75°F:

  • Seated, light work: 245 sensible, 205 latent
  • Office work: 250 sensible, 200 latent
  • Light assembly: 275 sensible, 275 latent

Cooling Load from Lights

Q = 3.41 × W × FU × SA

Where:

  • Q = Heat gain from lights (BTU/hr)
  • 3.41 = Conversion factor (BTU/hr per watt)
  • W = Installed lighting watts
  • FU = Usage factor (typically 1.0)
  • SA = Special allowance (typically 1.0, or 1.2 for recessed fixtures)

Example:

Calculate heat from 5,000 watts of recessed LED lighting:

  • Q = 3.41 × 5,000 × 1.0 × 1.2 = 20,460 BTU/hr

Solar Heat Gain through Glass

Q = A × SHGC × SC × CLF

Where:

  • Q = Solar heat gain (BTU/hr)
  • A = Glass area (ft²)
  • SHGC = Solar heat gain coefficient
  • SC = Shading coefficient
  • CLF = Cooling load factor

Note: SHGC ranges from 0 to 1. Lower values indicate better solar control. Typical values: clear glass 0.7-0.8, low-e glass 0.2-0.4

Ventilation Load

Qs = 1.08 × CFM × ΔT

Ql = 0.68 × CFM × Δω

Where:

  • Qs = Sensible ventilation load (BTU/hr)
  • Ql = Latent ventilation load (BTU/hr)
  • CFM = Outdoor air ventilation rate
  • ΔT = Temperature difference (outdoor - indoor)
  • Δω = Humidity ratio difference (grains/lb)

Duct Sizing Calculations

Equal Friction Method

D = 1.3 × (Q / V)0.625

Where:

  • D = Duct diameter (inches)
  • Q = Airflow rate (CFM)
  • V = Velocity (FPM)

Recommended velocities:

  • Main ducts: 800-1,200 FPM
  • Branch ducts: 600-900 FPM
  • Final branches: 400-600 FPM

Rectangular to Round Equivalent Diameter

Deq = 1.3 × (a × b)0.625 / (a + b)0.25

Where:

  • Deq = Equivalent round diameter (inches)
  • a = Duct width (inches)
  • b = Duct height (inches)

Example:

Find equivalent diameter for 24" × 18" duct:

  • Deq = 1.3 × (24 × 18)0.625 / (24 + 18)0.25 = 23.6 inches

Pressure Loss in Ducts

ΔP = (f × L × V²) / (2 × D × 4,005)

Where:

  • ΔP = Pressure loss (in. w.g. per 100 ft)
  • f = Friction factor
  • L = Length (ft)
  • V = Velocity (FPM)
  • D = Diameter (inches)

Note: Use duct calculators or friction charts for practical design. Typical friction: 0.08-0.15 in. w.g. per 100 ft.

Pipe Sizing Calculations

Pipe Size from Flow Rate

D = √(4Q / (πV × 448.83))

Where:

  • D = Inside diameter (ft)
  • Q = Flow rate (GPM)
  • V = Velocity (ft/s)
  • 448.83 = Conversion factor

Recommended velocities:

  • Chilled water: 4-10 ft/s
  • Hot water: 4-8 ft/s
  • Condenser water: 5-12 ft/s

Hazen-Williams Formula

V = 1.318 × C × R0.63 × S0.54

Where:

  • V = Velocity (ft/s)
  • C = Roughness coefficient (150 for steel, 130 for older pipes)
  • R = Hydraulic radius (ft)
  • S = Slope of energy grade line (ft/ft)

Pressure Drop (Simplified)

ΔP = (4 × f × L × V²) / (2 × g × D)

Where:

  • ΔP = Pressure drop (ft of head)
  • f = Friction factor
  • L = Pipe length (ft)
  • V = Velocity (ft/s)
  • g = Gravity (32.2 ft/s²)
  • D = Inside diameter (ft)

Note: For water systems, typical design: 4 ft head/100 ft pipe

Pump & Fan Power Calculations

Pump Hydraulic Power

HP = (GPM × Head × SG) / (3,960 × η)

Where:

  • HP = Horsepower
  • GPM = Flow rate (gallons per minute)
  • Head = Total head (feet)
  • SG = Specific gravity (1.0 for water)
  • η = Pump efficiency (decimal, typically 0.70-0.85)
  • 3,960 = Constant

Example:

Calculate HP for 500 GPM pump, 80 ft head, 75% efficiency:

  • HP = (500 × 80 × 1.0) / (3,960 × 0.75) = 13.5 HP
  • Select 15 HP motor

Fan Power (Airside)

HP = (CFM × SP) / (6,356 × η)

Where:

  • HP = Horsepower
  • CFM = Airflow rate (cubic feet per minute)
  • SP = Static pressure (in. w.g.)
  • η = Fan efficiency (decimal, typically 0.60-0.75)
  • 6,356 = Constant

Example:

Calculate fan HP for 20,000 CFM, 4" w.g., 65% efficiency:

  • HP = (20,000 × 4) / (6,356 × 0.65) = 19.4 HP
  • Select 20 HP motor

Total Head (Pumps)

Htotal = Hstatic + Hfriction + Hequipment

Where:

  • Htotal = Total head (ft)
  • Hstatic = Elevation difference (ft)
  • Hfriction = Pipe friction losses (ft)
  • Hequipment = Equipment pressure drops (ft)

Note: Convert equipment pressure drops: PSI × 2.31 = ft of water head

Motor Power Consumption

kW = HP × 0.746 / ηmotor

Where:

  • kW = Electrical power consumption
  • HP = Motor horsepower
  • 0.746 = Conversion factor (kW per HP)
  • ηmotor = Motor efficiency (typically 0.90-0.95)

Common Unit Conversions

Energy & Power

  • 1 ton = 12,000 BTU/hr
  • 1 kW = 3,412 BTU/hr
  • 1 HP = 2,545 BTU/hr
  • 1 HP = 0.746 kW
  • 1 BTU = 1,055 Joules
  • 1 kWh = 3,412 BTU

Pressure

  • 1 PSI = 2.31 ft H₂O
  • 1 PSI = 27.7 in. H₂O
  • 1 PSI = 6,895 Pa
  • 1 in. w.g. = 0.036 PSI
  • 1 in. w.g. = 249 Pa
  • 1 atm = 14.696 PSI

Flow Rates

  • 1 GPM = 0.002228 ft³/s
  • 1 GPM = 0.0631 L/s
  • 1 CFM = 0.4719 L/s
  • 1 CFM = 1.699 m³/hr
  • 500 GPM ≈ 1 ft³/s
  • 448.83 GPM = 1 ft³/s

Temperature

  • °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
  • °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9
  • °R = °F + 459.67
  • K = °C + 273.15
  • ΔT (°F) = ΔT (°C) × 1.8

Length & Area

  • 1 inch = 25.4 mm
  • 1 foot = 0.3048 m
  • 1 ft² = 0.0929 m²
  • 1 ft² = 144 in²
  • 1 acre = 43,560 ft²

Density & Specific Heat

  • Air: 0.075 lb/ft³ @ STP
  • Water: 62.4 lb/ft³ @ 60°F
  • Water: 8.34 lb/gal
  • Air cp: 0.24 BTU/lb·°F
  • Water cp: 1.0 BTU/lb·°F
