Air Handling Units (AHU)

Key Specifications

Air handling units are central to HVAC systems, conditioning and distributing air throughout buildings.

AHU Standard Specifications

Parameter Typical Range Notes
Airflow 2,000 - 100,000 CFM Varies by application and building size
External Static Pressure 1.0 - 6.0 in. w.g. Higher for longer duct runs
Face Velocity (Filter) 300 - 500 FPM Lower extends filter life
Face Velocity (Coil) 400 - 600 FPM Higher can cause carryover
Fan Motor HP 1 - 100 HP Size based on airflow and ESP
Cabinet Leakage < 2% at 4 in. w.g. Per ASHRAE 111 testing

Selection Criteria

  • Fan Type: Plenum, plug, or draw-through configuration
  • Filter Efficiency: MERV 8 (standard) to MERV 13+ (healthcare)
  • Heating Coil: Hot water, steam, or electric
  • Cooling Coil: Chilled water or DX refrigerant
  • Controls: VFD for energy efficiency, DDC integration
  • Access: Adequate access doors for maintenance

Important Considerations

Water-cooled chillers require cooling towers and condenser water pumps. Air-cooled chillers are easier to install but less efficient. Consider local climate and water availability when selecting chiller type.

Condensing vs Non-Condensing

Condensing boilers achieve higher efficiency by extracting heat from flue gases, but require corrosion-resistant materials. Best suited for systems with return water temperatures below 130°F.

Pump Selection Guidelines

  • Efficiency: Select pumps operating at 70-85% of BEP (Best Efficiency Point)
  • NPSH: Net Positive Suction Head Available must exceed NPSH Required
  • Motors: Premium efficiency motors, typically 1800 RPM for reliability
  • VFDs: Variable frequency drives for energy savings on variable flow systems
  • Redundancy: Consider standby pumps for critical systems
  • Materials: Bronze or stainless steel impellers for water service

Key Terms

Approach: Difference between leaving water temp and entering wet bulb temp.

Range: Difference between entering and leaving water temperatures.

Cycles of Concentration: Ratio of dissolved solids in recirculating water vs makeup water.

VAV Box Types and Specifications

Fan-Powered Parallel 300 - 5,000 Energy efficient, variable volume

Selection Criteria

  • Minimum Airflow: 30-50% of maximum for ventilation and stability
  • Pressure Rating: Match to system design static pressure
  • Reheat Type: Hot water (preferred), electric, or steam
  • Controls: DDC with airflow measurement and temperature control
  • Sound Level: NC 25-35 for occupied spaces
  • Turndown Ratio: Ability to modulate from max to min airflow

Duct Leakage Requirements

Per ASHRAE 90.1, duct leakage must be tested and sealed to meet maximum leakage rates. Seal class depends on duct pressure class and location (conditioned vs unconditioned space).
