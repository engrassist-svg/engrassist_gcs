Overview

Fixture schedules are essential tools for plumbing system design, providing standardized values for water demand and drainage loads. These values, established by plumbing codes and industry standards, enable engineers to properly size piping, pumps, water heaters, and treatment equipment.

Types of Fixture Unit Values

  • Water Supply Fixture Units (WSFU): Represent water demand for sizing supply piping
  • Drainage Fixture Units (DFU): Represent discharge load for sizing drain and vent piping
  • Flow Rate (GPM): Actual flow rate in gallons per minute at fixture

Water Supply Fixture Units (WSFU)

Water Supply Fixture Units are based on the Hunter Curve method developed by Roy B. Hunter in 1940. These values account for the probability that not all fixtures will be used simultaneously.

Drainage Fixture Units (DFU)

Drainage Fixture Units represent the drainage load from fixtures and are used to size drain, waste, and vent piping. DFU values are typically lower than WSFU values because drainage considers only the discharge load.
