  • Smooth (0.00015-0.0002 ft): Lowest friction loss, best for long duct runs and energy efficiency
  • Medium (0.00032-0.0005 ft): Standard commercial ductwork, good balance of cost and performance
  • Rough (0.003-0.005 ft): Higher friction loss, requires more fan power
  • Very Rough (0.01-0.015 ft): Highest friction loss, typically avoided in HVAC design when possible

Design Guidelines & Recommendations

ASHRAE Design Criteria

  • Velocity Limits: Supply ducts 500-900 fpm, return ducts 500-700 fpm, main ducts up to 1200 fpm
  • Friction Rate: Typically 0.0067-0.0125 ft/100 ft for energy efficiency
  • Aspect Ratio: Rectangular ducts should not exceed 4:1 (preferably 3:1 or less)
  • Minimum Sizes: 6" minimum dimension for supply, 8" for return ducts
  • Noise Control: Lower velocities in noise-sensitive areas (400-600 fpm)

SMACNA Design Considerations

  • Pressure Classes: Select appropriate ductwork pressure class based on system operating pressure
  • Sealing Requirements: Consider leakage class requirements (Seal Class A, B, or C)
  • Support Spacing: Adequate structural support per SMACNA guidelines
  • Insulation: Account for external insulation thickness in space planning
  • Access: Provide access doors for cleaning and maintenance
Additional Considerations: Always account for pressure losses through fittings, transitions, dampers, and equipment. Total system pressure drop includes ductwork friction plus all components. Consider acoustical requirements, energy efficiency, constructability, and maintenance access in final design.
