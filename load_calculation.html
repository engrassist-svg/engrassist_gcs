Load Calculation Guidelines

Industry Standards

This calculator follows procedures based on Carrier Load Estimation methods and ACCA Manual J (Residential Load Calculation) and Manual N (Commercial Load Calculation) principles.

üìè

Design Conditions

Summer: Use ASHRAE 1% design dry bulb and mean coincident wet bulb temperatures for your location.

Winter: Use ASHRAE 99% design dry bulb temperature (99% of hours are warmer than this).

Indoor: Typical comfort conditions are 75¬∞F/50% RH for cooling and 70¬∞F for heating.

ü™ü

Window Heat Gain

Windows are the largest source of cooling load in most buildings due to:

  • Solar radiation (varies by orientation)
  • Conduction through glass
  • Poor insulation value

Best Practice: Use Low-E double or triple pane windows to reduce load by 30-50%.

üß±

Wall & Roof Insulation

Walls: R-13 to R-21 for frame construction, higher for cold climates.

Roofs: R-30 minimum, R-38 to R-49 recommended for attics.

Color Impact: Dark colors absorb 20-40% more heat than light colors on roofs and walls.

üë•

Internal Loads

People (per ASHRAE Table 1):

  • Seated, light work: 450 BTU/hr (250S + 200L)
  • Standing, walking: 500 BTU/hr (250S + 250L)
  • Moderate activity: 650 BTU/hr (295S + 355L)

Lighting: LED = 3.41 BTU/hr per Watt. Modern offices: 0.6-1.0 W/ft¬≤.

Equipment: Computers ~200 W, monitors ~50 W. Use actual nameplate data when available.

üí®

Infiltration & Ventilation

Infiltration: Uncontrolled air leakage through building envelope. Tight construction = 0.35 ACH, loose = 1.0 ACH.

Ventilation: Required outdoor air per ASHRAE 62.1/62.2. Typical office: 15-20 CFM per person.

üéØ

Equipment Sizing (ACCA Manual S)

Critical: Don't oversize! Oversized units short-cycle, don't dehumidify properly, waste energy, and fail prematurely.

Cooling Size Range: 95-115% of calculated load (115-125% for heat pumps due to defrost)

Heating Size Range: 100-140% of calculated load

Airflow: Variable based on climate (see calculator results):

  • Humid: 350-375 CFM/ton (better dehumidification)
  • Moderate: 400 CFM/ton (standard)
  • Dry: 425-450 CFM/ton (maximum capacity)
  • Heat Pumps: 450-500 CFM/ton for heating
