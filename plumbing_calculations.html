Fixture Unit Calculations

Example 1: Residential Water Supply

Given:

Single-family residence with following fixtures:

  • 2 Water Closets (tank type)
  • 3 Lavatories
  • 2 Showers
  • 1 Bathtub
  • 1 Kitchen Sink
  • 1 Dishwasher
  • 1 Clothes Washer
  • 2 Hose Bibbs

Calculate: Total WSFU for hot and cold water

Solution:

Cold Water WSFU:

  • Water Closets: 2 × 2.5 = 5.0 WSFU
  • Lavatories: 3 × 0.5 = 1.5 WSFU
  • Showers: 2 × 1.0 = 2.0 WSFU
  • Bathtub: 1 × 1.0 = 1.0 WSFU
  • Kitchen Sink: 1 × 0.75 = 0.75 WSFU
  • Clothes Washer: 1 × 1.0 = 1.0 WSFU
  • Hose Bibbs: 2 × 2.5 = 5.0 WSFU
  • Total Cold: 16.25 WSFU

Hot Water WSFU:

  • Lavatories: 3 × 0.5 = 1.5 WSFU
  • Showers: 2 × 1.0 = 2.0 WSFU
  • Bathtub: 1 × 1.0 = 1.0 WSFU
  • Kitchen Sink: 1 × 0.75 = 0.75 WSFU
  • Dishwasher: 1 × 1.4 = 1.4 WSFU
  • Clothes Washer: 1 × 1.0 = 1.0 WSFU
  • Total Hot: 7.65 WSFU

Convert to GPM (using Hunter's Curve/Code Tables):

  • 16.25 WSFU (cold) ≈ 13 GPM
  • 7.65 WSFU (hot) ≈ 7 GPM

Flow Rate and Velocity

Example 2: Calculate Velocity in Pipe

Given:

  • Flow Rate: 10 GPM
  • Pipe: 3/4" Type L Copper (ID = 0.785")

Calculate: Flow velocity in ft/s

Formula:

Velocity (fps) = (0.408 × GPM) / (ID²)

Where ID is in inches

Solution:

Velocity = (0.408 × 10) / (0.785²)

Velocity = 4.08 / 0.616

Velocity = 6.6 fps

Evaluation: This is within acceptable range (5-8 fps for cold water)

Example 3: Calculate Required Pipe Size

Given:

  • Required Flow: 15 GPM
  • Maximum Velocity: 8 fps (cold water)
  • Pipe Material: Type L Copper

Calculate: Minimum pipe size

Formula (rearranged):

ID = √[(0.408 × GPM) / Velocity]

Solution:

ID = √[(0.408 × 15) / 8]

ID = √[6.12 / 8]

ID = √0.765

ID = 0.875"

Result: Type L Copper 3/4" (ID = 0.785") is too small

Select: 1" Type L Copper (ID = 1.025") ✓

Verify: V = (0.408 × 15) / (1.025²) = 5.8 fps ✓

Pressure Loss Calculations

Example 4: Elevation Pressure Loss

Given:

  • Supply Pressure at ground floor: 60 psi
  • Fixture location: 3rd floor, 30 feet above supply

Calculate: Available pressure at 3rd floor fixture

Formula:

Pressure Loss = 0.433 psi/ft × Height (ft)

Solution:

Pressure Loss = 0.433 × 30 = 13.0 psi

Available Pressure = 60 - 13 = 47 psi

Evaluation: 47 psi is adequate for typical fixtures (need 15-20 psi minimum)

Example 5: Total Pressure Drop

Given:

  • Supply pressure: 60 psi
  • Elevation rise: 25 ft
  • Friction loss in piping: 8 psi
  • Water meter loss: 5 psi
  • PRV set point: 50 psi (reduces from 60 to 50)

Calculate: Available pressure at fixture

Solution:

Starting Pressure: 60 psi

After PRV: 50 psi

Less meter loss: 50 - 5 = 45 psi

Less elevation: 45 - (0.433 × 25) = 45 - 10.8 = 34.2 psi

Less friction: 34.2 - 8 = 26.2 psi

Evaluation: 26.2 psi is adequate for most fixtures

Drainage Sizing

Example 6: Size Branch Drain

Given:

Branch serves following fixtures:

  • 1 Water Closet (tank): 4 DFU
  • 1 Lavatory: 1 DFU
  • 1 Bathtub: 2 DFU

Slope: 1/4" per foot

Calculate: Minimum branch drain size

Solution:

Total DFU = 4 + 1 + 2 = 7 DFU

From IPC Table 710.1(2):

At 1/4" per foot slope:

  • 1-1/2" pipe: Max 3 DFU ✗
  • 2" pipe: Max 6 DFU ✗
  • 3" pipe: Max 20 DFU ✓

Result: 3" minimum branch drain size

Note: Water closet requires minimum 3" drain regardless of DFU count

Gas Pipe Sizing

Example 7: Natural Gas Pipe Sizing

Given:

Appliances on gas line:

  • Furnace: 100,000 BTU/hr
  • Water Heater: 40,000 BTU/hr
  • Range: 65,000 BTU/hr

Longest run: 60 feet

Gas: Natural gas, 0.60 specific gravity

Pressure drop: 0.5" water column maximum

Calculate: Minimum pipe size

Solution:

Total Load = 100,000 + 40,000 + 65,000 = 205,000 BTU/hr

From IPC Table 402.4(2) - Schedule 40 Steel Pipe:

For 60 feet length, 0.5" w.c. drop:

  • 3/4" pipe: 115,000 BTU/hr capacity ✗
  • 1" pipe: 219,000 BTU/hr capacity ✓

Result: 1" black steel pipe minimum

Water Heater Sizing

Example 8: Residential Tank Water Heater

Given:

4-bedroom house, 4 occupants

Peak hour demand from fixtures:

  • 2 Showers: 20 gallons each = 40 gal
  • 1 Clothes washer load: 15 gallons
  • Dishwasher: 6 gallons
  • Handwashing: 4 gallons

Calculate: Required first-hour rating

Solution:

Total Peak Hour Demand = 40 + 15 + 6 + 4 = 65 gallons/hour

Select water heater with First-Hour Rating (FHR) ≥ 65 gallons

Example: 50-gallon gas water heater with recovery rate of 40 GPH

FHR = 0.7 × Tank Size + Recovery Rate

FHR = 0.7 × 50 + 40 = 35 + 40 = 75 gallons ✓

Result: 50-gallon gas water heater adequate
