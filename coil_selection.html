Coil Selection Guidelines

Heat Transfer Equations

Air Side (Sensible Heat)

Q = 1.08 × CFM × ΔTair
  • Q = Heat transfer (BTU/hr)
  • CFM = Airflow (cubic feet per minute)
  • ΔTair = Air temperature difference (°F)
  • 1.08 = Constant (0.075 lb/ft³ × 60 min/hr × 0.24 BTU/lb·°F)

Fluid Side

Q = 500 × GPM × ΔTfluid × SG × Cp
  • Q = Heat transfer (BTU/hr)
  • GPM = Fluid flow (gallons per minute)
  • ΔTfluid = Fluid temperature difference (°F)
  • SG = Specific gravity of fluid
  • Cp = Specific heat (BTU/lb·°F)
  • 500 = Constant (8.34 lb/gal × 60 min/hr)

Typical Design Conditions

Heating Coils

  • Hot Water Supply: 180°F (typical), can range from 140°F to 200°F
  • Hot Water Return: 160°F (typical 20°F ΔT)
  • Entering Air: Varies with outdoor conditions (0°F to 70°F typical)
  • Leaving Air: 90-110°F for comfort heating
  • Face Velocity: 400-800 FPM typical
  • Air Temperature Rise: 40-70°F typical

Cooling Coils (Chilled Water)

  • Chilled Water Supply: 42-45°F (typical 44°F)
  • Chilled Water Return: 54-58°F (typical 10-14°F ΔT)
  • Entering Air: 75-80°F DB, varies with application
  • Leaving Air: 52-58°F typical (for comfort cooling)
  • Face Velocity: 300-500 FPM to prevent condensate carryover
  • Air Temperature Drop: 18-28°F typical

Glycol Solutions

  • Propylene Glycol: Food-grade, safer for potable water applications
  • Ethylene Glycol: Better heat transfer, toxic - used in closed loops only
  • Typical Concentration: 20-40% by weight for freeze protection
  • 30% Glycol: Freeze point approximately 5°F (propylene) or -10°F (ethylene)
  • Flow Rate Impact: 10-20% higher GPM needed vs. water for same capacity
  • Pump Power: Increases with glycol concentration due to higher viscosity

Design Tips

  • Approach Temperature: Minimum 5-8°F between leaving air and entering fluid temperature
  • Air Velocity: Lower velocities for cooling coils to prevent moisture carryover
  • Rows Deep: 3-4 rows for heating coils, 4-8 rows for cooling coils
  • Fin Spacing: 8-10 FPI for heating, 10-14 FPI for cooling (clean applications)
  • Pressure Drop: Target 0.3-0.8" W.C. air side, 5-15 ft water head fluid side
  • Coil Orientation: Counterflow preferred for best heat transfer efficiency

⚠️ Important Disclaimer

These calculations provide estimates for preliminary sizing only. Actual coil selection requires consideration of factors including humidity, altitude, fin spacing, number of rows, circuiting, pressure drop, freeze protection, and specific application requirements. Always consult with a licensed professional engineer and coil manufacturer for final selection. This calculator assumes sensible heat transfer only and does not account for latent cooling loads.
