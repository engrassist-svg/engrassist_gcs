Load Calculation Information

NEC Article 220 - Load Calculations

Electrical load calculations determine the size of electrical services and feeders. The NEC provides specific calculation methods with demand factors that account for diversity in electrical usage.

Commercial Load Calculations

Based on NEC 220.12 and 220.42, commercial loads include:

  • General Lighting: VA per square foot based on occupancy type
  • Receptacles: Often included in general lighting load
  • HVAC: At 100% of nameplate rating
  • Motors: 125% of largest motor plus 100% of others
  • Demand Factors: Applied to lighting per Table 220.42

Residential Load Calculations

NEC 220.82 Optional Method for dwelling units:

  • General Lighting: 3 VA/sq ft
  • Small Appliance: 1500 VA per circuit (minimum 2)
  • Laundry: 1500 VA per circuit
  • Demand Factor: First 10 kVA @ 100%, remainder @ 40%
  • HVAC: Larger of heating or cooling @ 100%
  • Other Appliances: @ 100%

Standard Service Sizes

Residential: 100A, 125A, 150A, 200A, 320A, 400A

Commercial: 100A, 200A, 400A, 600A, 800A, 1000A, 1200A, 1600A, 2000A

Always size services and feeders for the calculated load plus 20-25% for future expansion.

Load Calculation Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Undersized Service

Symptoms: Main breaker trips, cannot add new loads, low voltage

Solutions:

  • Re-calculate with all current loads
  • Apply proper demand factors per NEC
  • Upgrade service size if needed
  • Consider load management systems

Oversized Service

Symptoms: Excessive installation cost, high utility demand charges

Solutions:

  • Review calculation method
  • Verify demand factors were applied
  • Consider optional calculation methods

Calculation Checklist

  • ☐ All loads identified and quantified
  • ☐ Correct VA/sq ft for occupancy type
  • ☐ Demand factors properly applied
  • ☐ Motors at 125% of largest plus others
  • ☐ HVAC at larger of heating or cooling
  • ☐ Future loads considered (20-25%)
  • ☐ Voltage matches service voltage
