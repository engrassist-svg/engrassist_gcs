Overview

Selecting appropriate plumbing materials is critical for system performance, longevity, and code compliance. This guide covers specifications for common plumbing materials used in the United States, with references to major manufacturers and applicable standards.

Copper Pipe & Fittings

Copper has been the industry standard for water supply piping for over 80 years. It offers excellent durability, reliability, and is naturally antimicrobial.

Type M Thinnest Low-pressure applications, cost-sensitive projects Up to 200+ psi

Copper Fittings

Wrought Copper Fittings (ASME B16.22)

  • Material: C12200 copper (99.9% pure)
  • Joining Method: Soldered (brazed for high temp)
  • Pressure Rating: Matches pipe pressure rating
  • Types: Elbows (90°, 45°), tees, couplings, caps, adapters
  • Temperature Rating: 250°F for water, 406°F for steam

Press-Fit Copper Fittings

  • Manufacturers: Viega ProPress, Nibco Press System, Apollo Xpress
  • Joining Method: Mechanical press connection (no flame)
  • Installation Time: 60-80% faster than soldering
  • Applications: Commercial, retrofit, occupied buildings
  • Standards: ASSE 1061, IAPMO/ANSI LC-4

PEX Tubing Systems

Cross-Linked Polyethylene (PEX) has gained significant market share due to its flexibility, ease of installation, and resistance to corrosion and freezing.

PEX Types (ASTM F876/F877)

Type Manufacturing Process Characteristics Best For
PEX-A Peroxide (Engel method) Most flexible, best cold weather performance, expandable Freeze-prone areas, tight bends
PEX-B Silane moisture cure Moderately flexible, most common, cost-effective General residential plumbing
PEX-C E-beam irradiation Least flexible, lower cost Straight runs, budget projects

PEX Specifications

Physical Properties

  • Temperature Rating: 200°F at 80 psi, 180°F at 100 psi
  • Pressure Rating: 160 psi @ 73°F, 100 psi @ 180°F
  • Lifespan: 50+ years (estimated based on testing)
  • UV Resistance: Not UV stable - indoor use only
  • Freeze Resistance: Expands without bursting
  • Color Coding: Red (hot), Blue (cold), White (either)

PEX Connection Methods

Crimp/Clamp Method

Tool: Crimp ring tool

Fittings: Copper or brass insert fittings

Pros: Low cost, simple, reliable

Cons: Reduces ID slightly

Brands: Zurn, Viega, Uponor

Expansion Method

Tool: Expansion tool (manual or powered)

Fittings: PEX-A specific expansion rings

Pros: No ID reduction, best flow

Cons: Higher tool cost, PEX-A only

Brands: Uponor (Wirsbo), Rehau

Push-Fit Method

Tool: None required

Fittings: Push-to-connect fittings

Pros: No tools, fast, removable

Cons: Higher fitting cost

Brands: SharkBite, Tectite, BlueFin

Press Method

Tool: Press tool

Fittings: Press fittings with EPDM seals

Pros: Fast, no flame, commercial grade

Cons: Expensive tool

Brands: Viega, Sioux Chief

CPVC Pipe Systems

Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride (CPVC) is approved for hot and cold water distribution and is widely used as a cost-effective alternative to copper.

CPVC Specifications (ASTM D2846)

Physical Properties

  • Temperature Rating: 180-200°F at 100 psi
  • Pressure Rating: 400 psi @ 73°F, 100 psi @ 180°F
  • Sizes: CTS (Copper Tube Size) - 1/2" to 2"
  • Schedule 80: Available for higher pressure applications
  • Joining: Solvent cement welding
  • Color: Cream/beige (for hot/cold water)

DWV (Drain-Waste-Vent) Materials

PVC DWV Pipe (ASTM D2665, D3034)

  • Schedule 40: Standard for DWV applications
  • Sizes: 1-1/4" to 12" and larger
  • Color: White (DWV), Green (sewer)
  • Joining: Solvent cement welding
  • Temperature: Maximum 140°F
  • Manufacturers: Charlotte Pipe, IPEX, JM Eagle, Genova

ABS Pipe (ASTM D2661)

  • Material: Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene
  • Color: Black
  • Applications: DWV systems (not allowed in all jurisdictions)
  • Joining: Solvent cement (single-step ABS cement)
  • Pros: Easier to work with in cold weather vs PVC
  • Cons: Not as rigid as PVC, warps more easily

Cast Iron Soil Pipe (ASTM A888, CISPI 301)

  • Types: Service Weight (SV), Extra Heavy (XH)
  • Joining: No-hub (rubber couplings) or hub-and-spigot
  • Applications: Commercial buildings, multi-family, sound control
  • Advantages: Superior sound dampening, fire resistance
  • Manufacturers: Charlotte Pipe, Tyler Pipe, AB&I Foundry
  • Couplings: Mission, Fernco, Husky
