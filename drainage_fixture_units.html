About Drainage Fixture Units (DFU)

Drainage Fixture Units represent the probable discharge of fixtures into the drainage system. DFU values account for the volume and duration of discharge. Use total DFU to size horizontal branches, vertical stacks, building drains, and building sewers.

Understanding Drainage Fixture Units

What are Drainage Fixture Units (DFU)?

Drainage Fixture Units (DFU) are standardized values that represent the probable discharge flow from plumbing fixtures into the drainage system. Like WSFU for water supply, DFU accounts for the fact that not all fixtures discharge simultaneously, allowing realistic drainage system sizing.

DFU vs. WSFU

  • WSFU: Water Supply Fixture Units - for sizing water distribution piping
  • DFU: Drainage Fixture Units - for sizing drainage and vent piping
  • Values differ because drainage considers discharge volume and duration
  • DFU values are generally higher than WSFU values for the same fixture

Standard DFU Values (IPC/UPC)

  • Water Closet (Private): 3 DFU
  • Water Closet (Public): 6 DFU
  • Lavatory (Private): 1 DFU
  • Lavatory (Public): 2 DFU
  • Shower: 2 DFU
  • Bathtub: 2 DFU
  • Kitchen Sink: 2 DFU
  • Dishwasher (Domestic): 2 DFU
  • Clothes Washer (2"): 3 DFU
  • Floor Drain (2"): 2 DFU
  • Urinal: 4 DFU
  • Service Sink: 3 DFU

Using DFU for Pipe Sizing

Total DFU is used to size various components of the drainage system:

Horizontal Branches

Branch drains collecting discharge from multiple fixtures. Size based on total DFU and slope (typically 1/4" per foot minimum).

Stacks (Vertical Drainage)

Vertical pipes receiving discharge from horizontal branches. Soil stacks receive toilets; waste stacks receive other fixtures. Sized based on total DFU on stack and number of stories.

Building Drain

Horizontal pipe inside building collecting discharge from stacks and branches. Sized based on total DFU and slope (1/4" per foot typical).

Building Sewer

Pipe from building drain to public sewer or septic tank. Typically same size or larger than building drain. Minimum slope requirements apply.

Slope Requirements

  • Pipes 2-1/2" and smaller: Minimum 1/4" per foot slope
  • Pipes 3" and larger: Minimum 1/8" per foot slope (1/4" preferred)
  • Building sewer: Minimum 1/4" per foot for pipes under 6"
  • Maximum slope: Generally limited to prevent solids separation

Special Considerations

  • Continuous Flow: Some fixtures (commercial dishwashers, certain processes) have continuous discharge requiring special sizing
  • Grease Interceptors: Required for food service - add to DFU calculation
  • Multiple Dwellings: Additional diversity factors may apply for large buildings
  • Commercial Kitchens: Often require larger pipes than DFU indicates due to grease and food solids

Drainage System Troubleshooting

Common Drainage Problems

Symptom Possible Causes Diagnostic Steps Solution
Slow Drainage Partial blockage, undersized pipe, improper slope, inadequate venting Snake drain, verify pipe size vs. DFU, check slope with level, test venting Clear blockage, upsize pipe if undersized, correct slope, improve venting
Frequent Backups Undersized drainage, chronic blockage, insufficient slope, vent issues Calculate actual DFU vs. pipe capacity, camera inspection, verify slope, check vents Upsize drainage pipes, regular maintenance, correct slope, add venting
Gurgling Sounds Inadequate venting, partial blockage, air intake issues Verify vent system, check for blockages, inspect AAVs if present Install proper venting, clear blockages, replace faulty AAVs
Overflowing Cleanouts Complete blockage downstream, collapsed pipe, roots in sewer Camera inspection, snake from cleanout, pressure test if necessary Clear blockage, excavate and replace damaged section, root treatment

Prevention Best Practices

  • Size drainage pipes properly based on total DFU during design
  • Maintain proper slope throughout installation (verify with levels)
  • Install cleanouts at strategic locations for maintenance access
  • Provide adequate venting for all drainage components
  • Use proper fittings (long-sweep elbows, wyes, not short 90s)
  • Regular maintenance and inspection, especially for commercial buildings
  • Educate building occupants on proper disposal practices

Professional Disclaimer

Drainage fixture unit values and sizing requirements vary by local plumbing code. This calculator uses typical IPC values. Always verify DFU values and pipe sizing tables with adopted local codes. Actual drainage capacity depends on pipe material, slope, length, fittings, and installation quality. For complex systems or commercial buildings, consult a licensed professional engineer or plumber.
