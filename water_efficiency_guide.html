Overview

Water efficiency in plumbing systems reduces water consumption, lowers utility costs, and minimizes environmental impact. Modern plumbing codes increasingly mandate water-efficient fixtures and systems.

Benefits of Water-Efficient Plumbing

  • Reduced Water Bills: Lower consumption means lower costs
  • Energy Savings: Less hot water used reduces energy for heating
  • Environmental Impact: Conserves freshwater resources
  • Infrastructure: Reduces strain on water treatment and delivery systems
  • Code Compliance: Meets increasingly strict efficiency standards
  • Building Certifications: LEED, Green Globes, Living Building Challenge

High-Efficiency Fixtures

WaterSense Program

EPA WaterSense is a voluntary program that labels water-efficient products meeting strict performance and efficiency criteria.

  • Certification: Third-party tested to meet EPA specifications
  • Performance: Must perform as well as or better than standard models
  • Savings: At least 20% more efficient than federal standards
  • Label: Look for WaterSense label when specifying fixtures

Hot Water Distribution Efficiency

Reducing Hot Water Waste

A significant amount of water is wasted while waiting for hot water to reach fixtures. Strategies to minimize waste:

  • Compact Layout: Group fixtures near water heater
  • Smaller Pipe Sizes: Reduce volume in pipes (balance with flow requirements)
  • Insulation: Minimum R-3 pipe insulation on hot water lines
  • Recirculation Systems: Keep hot water continuously available
  • Point-of-Use Heaters: Electric heaters at remote fixtures
  • Structured Plumbing: PEX home-run systems reduce pipe volume

Hot Water Recirculation

Continuous Recirculation

  • Design: Dedicated return line from last fixture to water heater
  • Pump: Small circulator pump (1/40 HP typical)
  • Controls: Timer, aquastat, or on-demand activation
  • Pros: Instant hot water, reduced waste
  • Cons: Energy cost for pump and heat loss
  • Best For: Large homes, commercial buildings

Demand Recirculation

  • Operation: Pump activated only when needed (button, motion sensor)
  • Return: Uses cold water line as return
  • Savings: 90% less energy vs. continuous operation
  • Products: Grundfos Comfort System, Taco SmartPlus

Alternative Water Sources

Graywater Systems

Graywater is wastewater from sinks, showers, and laundry that can be reused for irrigation or toilet flushing.

  • Sources: Showers, bathtubs, bathroom sinks, washing machines
  • Excluded: Kitchen sinks (grease), dishwashers, toilets (blackwater)
  • Treatment: Simple filtration for irrigation; advanced treatment for toilet flushing
  • Savings: 30-50% reduction in potable water use
  • Codes: California, Arizona, and other states have specific graywater codes
  • Systems: Simple laundry-to-landscape or complex treatment systems

Rainwater Harvesting

  • Collection: Roof catchment directed to storage cisterns
  • Storage: Above-ground or below-ground tanks (500-10,000+ gallons)
  • Treatment: Filtration, UV disinfection for non-potable use
  • Uses: Irrigation, toilet flushing, cooling towers, vehicle washing
  • Sizing: Based on roof area, rainfall data, demand
  • ROI: Typically 10-20 years depending on water costs

Condensate Recovery

Capture condensate from HVAC equipment and use for irrigation or cooling towers:

  • Sources: Cooling coils, condensing boilers, steam systems
  • Volume: Commercial HVAC can produce 10-100+ gallons/day
  • Quality: Relatively clean but slightly acidic (pH ~5-6)
  • Uses: Irrigation, cooling tower makeup, non-potable applications

Commercial Water Efficiency Strategies

Commercial Kitchen

  • Pre-Rinse Spray Valves: Max 1.3 gpm (WaterSense) vs. 3-5 gpm older models
  • ENERGY STAR Dishwashers: 30-50% less water than standard
  • Ice Machines: Specify air-cooled vs. water-cooled (saves 150-200 gpd)
  • Steam Cookers: Connectionless (closed loop) vs. boiler-based
  • Dipper Wells: Flow-through vs. recirculating models

Cooling Towers

  • Conductivity Controllers: Optimize blowdown based on water chemistry
  • Side-Stream Filtration: Reduce blowdown frequency
  • Water Treatment: Allow higher cycles of concentration
  • Submetering: Track cooling tower water use separately

Landscape Irrigation

  • Weather-Based Controllers: Adjust watering based on ET and rainfall
  • Drip Irrigation: 90%+ efficiency vs. 60% for spray systems
  • Separate Meters: Irrigation on separate meter (no sewer charges)
  • Zoning: Group plants by water needs
  • Native Plants: Select drought-tolerant, locally adapted species
