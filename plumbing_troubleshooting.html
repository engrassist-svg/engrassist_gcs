Systematic Troubleshooting Approach

Step-by-Step Diagnostic Process

1. Define the Problem

  • What is the specific symptom?
  • When did it start? (sudden vs. gradual)
  • Which fixtures or areas are affected?
  • Is it constant or intermittent?
  • Have there been recent changes or work done?

2. Gather Data

  • Measure water pressure at multiple locations
  • Test flow rates at affected fixtures
  • Check water heater settings and condition
  • Inspect visible piping for damage or corrosion
  • Review building plans if available

3. Isolate the Cause

  • Test upstream vs. downstream of problem area
  • Isolate sections using shutoff valves
  • Compare hot vs. cold water systems
  • Determine if municipal or building-specific
  • Eliminate possibilities systematically

4. Implement Solution

  • Select appropriate repair method
  • Obtain necessary parts and tools
  • Follow safety procedures
  • Make repairs systematically
  • Test thoroughly after repair

5. Verify and Document

  • Confirm problem is resolved
  • Test all affected fixtures
  • Monitor for recurrence
  • Document cause and solution
  • Consider preventive measures

Water Supply Issues

Low Water Pressure

Safety First

Before troubleshooting plumbing systems:

  • Shut off water supply where appropriate
  • Know location of main shutoff valve
  • Use appropriate PPE (safety glasses, gloves)
  • Be aware of scalding hazards with hot water
  • Call licensed plumber for gas systems or complex issues

Low Pressure at All Fixtures

Possible Causes:

  • Main valve partially closed
  • Pressure-reducing valve (PRV) set too low or failed
  • Undersized water service
  • Clogged water meter or filter
  • Municipal supply pressure issue
  • Corrosion/buildup in old galvanized pipes

Diagnostic Steps:

  1. Check main valve - ensure fully open
  2. Test pressure at meter and furthest fixture
  3. Inspect and test PRV if installed
  4. Contact neighbors to determine if municipal issue
  5. Check filters/strainers for clogs

Solutions:

  • Open main valve fully
  • Adjust or replace PRV
  • Clean or replace filters
  • Upgrade water service size if undersized
  • Install booster pump if municipal pressure inadequate

Low Pressure When Multiple Fixtures Run

Cause: Undersized piping for demand

Solutions:

  • Upsize main trunk lines
  • Increase PRV setting if possible
  • Reduce simultaneous fixture use
  • Install pressure booster system

Drainage Problems

Slow Drains

Single Fixture Drains Slowly

Likely Causes:

  • Partial clog in fixture drain
  • Hair/soap buildup in trap
  • Vent blockage causing slow drainage
  • Insufficient slope in drain pipe

Solutions:

  • Clean trap and drain with snake or auger
  • Chemical drain cleaners (use cautiously)
  • Inspect and clear vent if accessible
  • Professional hydro-jetting for stubborn clogs

Multiple Fixtures Drain Slowly

Likely Causes:

  • Blockage in main drain line
  • Vent system blockage or improper venting
  • Root intrusion in sewer line
  • Sag or belly in horizontal drain

Diagnostic Steps:

  • Determine which fixtures are affected (same branch?)
  • Check for gurgling sounds (vent issue indicator)
  • Inspect cleanouts for standing water
  • Video camera inspection for line condition

Drain Odors

Possible Causes:

  • Dry trap (P-trap has evaporated)
  • Cracked or broken vent pipe
  • Improperly vented fixture
  • Biofilm buildup in drains
  • Sewer gas leakage through damaged pipes

Solutions:

  • Pour water into drains to refill traps
  • Install trap primers for floor drains
  • Repair or replace broken vent pipes
  • Clean drains with enzyme treatment
  • Smoke test to locate sewer gas leaks

Plumbing Noises

Water Hammer

Description: Loud banging when valves close suddenly

Causes:

  • Quick-closing valves (solenoid valves on appliances)
  • High water pressure (>80 psi)
  • Loose pipes that can move
  • Failed or missing water hammer arrestors

Solutions:

  • Install water hammer arrestors at problem locations
  • Reduce system pressure with PRV
  • Secure loose pipes with proper supports
  • Replace worn valve washers
  • Install slow-closing valves where feasible

Whistling or Squealing

Causes:

  • High velocity flow (undersized pipes)
  • Partially closed valves
  • Worn valve washers
  • Resonance in piping system

Solutions:

  • Reduce pressure or upsize piping
  • Fully open all valves
  • Replace worn washers and seats
  • Install flow restrictors or pressure regulators

Leak Detection and Repair

Visible Leaks

Leaks at Joints and Connections

Common Locations:

  • Threaded connections (loose or worn tape/dope)
  • Compression fittings (loose nuts, worn ferrules)
  • Solder joints (pinhole leaks, cold solder joints)
  • Press fittings (incomplete press, damaged O-ring)
  • Flange connections (failed gaskets)

Repair Methods:

  • Threaded: Drain, disassemble, re-tape with Teflon tape or pipe dope, reassemble
  • Compression: Tighten nut; if leak persists, replace ferrule
  • Solder: Drain, heat joint, remove old solder, re-solder properly
  • Press: Cut out fitting, install new press fitting
  • Emergency: Pipe repair clamp as temporary fix

Hidden Leaks

Detection Methods

  • Water Meter Test: Turn off all water, check meter for movement
  • Staining/Damage: Look for water stains, mold, soft drywall
  • Acoustic Detection: Listen for running water with all fixtures off
  • Thermal Imaging: Infrared camera shows temperature differences
  • Moisture Meters: Detect moisture in walls and floors
  • Dye Testing: Food coloring in toilet tank (leak if color appears in bowl)

Gas System Issues

⚠️ GAS SAFETY WARNING

If you smell gas or suspect a gas leak:

  • DO NOT use any electrical switches, phones, or create sparks
  • EVACUATE the building immediately
  • CALL gas company or 911 from outside
  • DO NOT re-enter until cleared by professionals

Only licensed professionals should work on gas systems.

Low Gas Pressure

Symptoms: Appliances not heating properly, yellow flames (should be blue)

Possible Causes:

  • Partially closed gas valve
  • Undersized gas piping
  • Regulator failure
  • High demand from multiple appliances
  • Gas meter insufficient for load

Professional Solutions:

  • Pressure test system to confirm low pressure
  • Verify all valves fully open
  • Check/replace regulator
  • Upsize gas piping if needed
  • Coordinate with gas company for service upgrade

Diagnostic Tools and Testing

Essential Diagnostic Tools

Pressure Gauge

Test water pressure at various locations

Normal: 40-60 psi

Use: Attach to hose bibb or test valve

Flow Meter / Bucket Test

Measure actual flow rate (GPM)

Method: Time how long to fill 5-gallon bucket

Expected: 2-3 GPM typical for lavatory

Drain Snake/Auger

Clear drain clogs mechanically

Types: Hand-crank, drill-powered, motorized

Sizes: 1/4" to 3/4" diameter

Pipe Locator

Locate pipes behind walls

Types: Metal detector, electromagnetic, acoustic

Use: Find pipes before cutting

Advanced Diagnostic Equipment

Video Camera Inspection

Inspect inside drain and sewer lines

Identify blockages, cracks, root intrusion

Professional service or rental

Thermal Imaging Camera

Detect hidden leaks and moisture

Shows temperature differences

Identifies water damage behind walls

Acoustic Leak Detector

Listen for water flow in walls

Pinpoint leak locations

Professional-grade equipment

Smoke Testing Equipment

Locate sewer gas leaks

Pump non-toxic smoke into drainage

Smoke exits through leak points
