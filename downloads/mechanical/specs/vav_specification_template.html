VAV Terminal Units

This Word template will provide a complete CSI-formatted specification section for VAV terminal units. The template includes the following sections:

PART 1 - GENERAL

  • 1.1 Summary: Section includes VAV terminal units complete with controls
  • 1.2 Related Sections: Ductwork, Controls, Electrical
  • 1.3 References: ASHRAE 111, AHRI 880, NFPA 90A, UL 1995
  • 1.4 Submittals: Product data, shop drawings, O&M manuals, warranties
  • 1.5 Quality Assurance: Manufacturer qualifications, installer qualifications
  • 1.6 Delivery, Storage, and Handling: Packaging and protection requirements
  • 1.7 Warranty: 1-year manufacturer's warranty minimum

PART 2 - PRODUCTS

  • 2.1 Manufacturers:
    • Titus HVAC
    • Price Industries
    • Nailor Industries
    • Krueger
    • Metal Industries
    • Or approved equal
  • 2.2 VAV Terminal Units - Cooling Only:
    • Construction: 22-gauge galvanized steel casing minimum
    • Damper: Opposed-blade damper, galvanized steel blades
    • Actuator: 24VAC spring return modulating actuator
    • Airflow sensor: Factory-installed averaging pitot tube or hot wire sensor
    • Controller: Microprocessor-based DDC controller with BACnet/IP communication
    • Insulation: 1-inch fiberglass acoustic liner on interior surfaces
    • Sound rating: Not to exceed specified NC levels
  • 2.3 VAV Terminal Units - Hot Water Reheat:
    • All requirements of 2.2 above, plus:
    • Reheat coil: Copper tubes, aluminum fins
    • Coil construction: AHRI 410 certified
    • Working pressure: 150 psig minimum
    • Test pressure: 300 psig
    • Valve: 2-way modulating control valve, equal percentage characteristic
    • Valve actuator: 24VAC modulating with spring return
  • 2.4 VAV Terminal Units - Electric Reheat:
    • All requirements of 2.2 above, plus:
    • Heating elements: Enclosed tubular nickel-chromium resistance heaters
    • Staging: Minimum 2-stage control for capacities over 5 kW
    • Safety: Automatic reset thermal cutout, manual reset backup
    • Wattage: As scheduled
  • 2.5 Series Fan-Powered VAV Terminal Units:
    • All requirements above, plus:
    • Fan: Forward-curved centrifugal blower, statically and dynamically balanced
    • Motor: ECM motor with variable speed control
    • Filter: MERV 8 minimum, field-replaceable
    • Plenum connection: Factory-provided plenum inlet collar
  • 2.6 Controls:
    • Controller: Microprocessor-based with BACnet/IP or Modbus communication
    • Control sequence: Cooling with reheat as per sequence of operations
    • Points provided: Airflow, damper position, discharge air temp, valve/reheat position
    • Integration: Full integration with building automation system
  • 2.7 Accessories:
    • Sound attenuation: 1-inch fiberglass duct liner on interior
    • Flex duct connectors: 3-6 feet of insulated flex duct from box to diffuser
    • Volume dampers: Manual balancing dampers as required

PART 3 - EXECUTION

  • 3.1 Examination: Verify duct sizes and locations before installation
  • 3.2 Installation:
    • Install per manufacturer's instructions
    • Support from structure, not from ductwork
    • Install level and plumb
    • Maintain minimum straight duct runs per manufacturer (typically 3-5 diameters upstream)
    • Install access panels in ceiling for service access
    • Label each unit with zone designation
  • 3.3 Connections:
    • Ductwork: Connect with gaskets, seal all joints
    • Electrical: Per NEC, coordinate voltage with electrical drawings
    • Controls: Wire per control drawings, test communications
    • Hot water (if applicable): Pipe per plumbing drawings, provide isolation valves
  • 3.4 Testing:
    • Verify all units operational
    • Calibrate airflow sensors per manufacturer's instructions
    • Test damper operation through full stroke
    • Verify reheat operation (if applicable)
    • Test communication with building automation system
  • 3.5 Air Balancing:
    • Balance per ASHRAE 111
    • Verify minimum and maximum airflows
    • Document final settings
  • 3.6 Commissioning:
    • Functional performance testing per commissioning plan
    • Verify control sequences
    • Train owner's personnel on operation and maintenance
  • 3.7 Demonstration and Training: Provide 4 hours on-site training for facilities staff
