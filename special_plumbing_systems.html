Medical Gas Systems

Medical gas systems deliver life-sustaining gases to patient care areas in healthcare facilities. These systems are highly regulated and must comply with NFPA 99: Healthcare Facilities Code.

Common Medical Gases

Gas Color Code (US) Typical Pressure Applications
Medical Air Yellow 50-55 psig Respiration, pneumatic tools, ventilators
Oxygen (O₂) Green (US) / White (International) 50-55 psig Respiratory therapy, anesthesia
Nitrous Oxide (N₂O) Blue 50-55 psig Anesthesia, sedation
Nitrogen (N₂) Black 160-185 psig Power surgical tools, cryotherapy
Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) Gray 50-55 psig Insufflation (laparoscopic surgery)

System Requirements (NFPA 99)

Source Equipment

  • Medical Air: Oil-free compressors with dryers and filters
  • Oxygen: Bulk liquid oxygen or manifold cylinder system
  • Emergency Backup: Reserve supply for all critical gases
  • Alarms: Master alarm panels monitor pressures and reserves

Piping and Installation

  • Materials: Type K or L medical gas copper
  • Joining: Brazed with BCuP-5 (silver-phosphorus) filler
  • Cleaning: Oxygen-cleaned and sealed before installation
  • Testing: Pressure test, purge, and gas purity verification
  • Labeling: Color-coded labels every 20 feet
  • Zone Valves: Required for isolation by area

Medical Vacuum and WAGD

  • Medical Vacuum: 15-19 inches Hg for suctioning during procedures
  • WAGD (Waste Anesthetic Gas Disposal): Remove waste gases from OR
  • Equipment: Vacuum pumps, receivers, monitoring systems
  • Pipe Color: White for vacuum, Purple for WAGD

Laboratory Plumbing

Laboratory plumbing systems must handle corrosive chemicals, high-purity water, and specialty gases while maintaining safety and containment.

Laboratory Drainage

Acid Waste Systems

  • Materials: Borosilicate glass, polypropylene, PVDF, or special alloys
  • Applications: pH < 6 or containing corrosive chemicals
  • Neutralization: May require neutralization tank before discharge
  • Venting: Separate vent system to exterior
  • Standards: ASPE Plumbing Engineering Design Handbook Vol. 4

Solvent Waste Systems

  • Separation: Keep separate from aqueous waste
  • Materials: Chemical-resistant piping (polypropylene, PVDF)
  • Containment: Secondary containment for spill protection
  • Fire Safety: Fire-rated construction for solvent areas

Laboratory Water Systems

Pure Water Types

  • DI Water (Deionized): Ion exchange, <1 microsiemens conductivity
  • RO Water (Reverse Osmosis): Membrane filtration, 90-99% contaminant removal
  • Type I Water (Ultrapure): 18.2 megohm-cm resistivity for critical applications
  • Type II Water: 1-10 megohm-cm for general laboratory use
  • Distilled Water: Thermal distillation removes minerals

Laboratory Gas Distribution

  • Natural Gas: Bunsen burners, equipment (declining use)
  • Nitrogen: Inert atmosphere, instrumentation
  • Compressed Air: Instrumentation, pneumatics
  • Specialty Gases: Helium, argon, hydrogen (various applications)
  • Safety: Gas cabinets, monitoring, emergency shutoffs

Process Piping

Process piping systems convey fluids in industrial and commercial applications, often requiring specialized materials and design considerations.

Common Process Applications

Glycol Systems

Closed-loop heating/cooling

Materials: Steel, copper, stainless

Temperature: -20°F to 250°F

Cooling Water

Heat rejection, process cooling

Materials: PVC, CPVC, steel

Water treatment critical

Chemical Feed

Water treatment chemicals

Materials: PVC, CPVC, PVDF

Metering pumps and controls

Steam Systems

Process heating, humidification

Materials: Black steel, stainless

Condensate return required

Code and Standards

  • ASME B31.3: Process Piping Code
  • ASME B31.1: Power Piping Code
  • ASME Section VIII: Pressure Vessels
  • OSHA PSM: Process Safety Management (if handling hazardous chemicals)

Vacuum Systems

Applications

  • Medical: Surgical suction, patient care (15-19" Hg)
  • Laboratory: Filtration, distillation (20-29" Hg)
  • Dental: Oral evacuation systems (12-14" Hg)
  • Industrial: Material handling, packaging

System Components

  • Vacuum Pumps: Liquid ring, rotary vane, or scroll type
  • Receivers: Buffer tanks to reduce cycling
  • Controls: Pressure switches, sequence controllers
  • Piping: Copper or steel, sized for CFM at design vacuum
  • Separators: Remove liquids from vacuum stream

Compressed Air Systems

Applications and Pressure Levels

  • Instrument Air: Clean, dry air for controls (40-125 psig)
  • Plant Air: Pneumatic tools, manufacturing (80-100 psig)
  • Breathing Air: SCBA filling, emergency systems (2000-4500 psig)
  • Medical Air: See Medical Gas Systems above (50-55 psig)

Air Treatment

  • Filters: Remove particulates and oil aerosols
  • Dryers: Refrigerated or desiccant to remove moisture
  • Dew Point: -40°F typical for instrument air
  • Oil-Free: Required for medical, food, and some industrial uses

Fire Protection Systems

Fire protection plumbing includes automatic sprinkler systems, standpipes, and fire pumps designed per NFPA standards.

System Types

Wet Pipe Sprinklers

Pipes filled with water under pressure

Most common, fastest response

Not suitable for freezing areas

Dry Pipe Sprinklers

Pipes filled with compressed air/nitrogen

For unheated areas (freezing risk)

Slower activation than wet pipe

Preaction Systems

Combination dry pipe and detection

Requires two events to discharge

Used where accidental discharge costly

Deluge Systems

Open heads, actuation valve

All heads discharge simultaneously

High-hazard occupancies

Key Standards

  • NFPA 13: Installation of Sprinkler Systems
  • NFPA 14: Installation of Standpipe and Hose Systems
  • NFPA 20: Installation of Stationary Pumps for Fire Protection
  • NFPA 24: Installation of Private Fire Service Mains
  • NFPA 25: Inspection, Testing, and Maintenance
