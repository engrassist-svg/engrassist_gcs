About Building Codes & Standards

Building codes and standards are the foundation of safe, efficient, and compliant construction design. These regulations establish minimum requirements for building systems, materials, and design practices to protect public health, safety, and welfare.

In the construction design industry, engineers and designers must navigate multiple code jurisdictions and standards organizations. The International Code Council (ICC) publishes the International Building Code (IBC) and related mechanical, plumbing, electrical, and fire codes that serve as model codes adopted by most U.S. jurisdictions. Professional organizations like ASHRAE (American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers) develop consensus standards for HVAC design, while NFPA (National Fire Protection Association) provides fire and life safety codes.

Staying current with codes is essential as they are updated on regular cycles (typically every 3 years for ICC codes). Local jurisdictions may adopt specific code editions and amendments, so designers must verify applicable codes for each project location. Understanding Code Adoption

Building codes are adopted at the state, county, or municipal level. Each jurisdiction determines which code edition to adopt and may include local amendments. Always verify the applicable codes and amendments for your specific project location before beginning design work.

Code Compliance Best Practices

  • Verify jurisdiction: Confirm which codes and editions apply to your project location
  • Check amendments: Review local amendments that may modify model code requirements
  • Stay current: ICC codes update on a 3-year cycle; subscribe to code change notifications
  • Document compliance: Maintain code analysis documentation throughout design
  • Coordinate disciplines: Ensure mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and fire protection designs comply with all applicable codes
  • Early review: Engage code officials early in design for complex projects or alternative compliance methods
