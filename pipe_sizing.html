ASHRAE Pipe Sizing Standards

ASHRAE 90.1 Requirements

Modern Standards (2010+): ASHRAE 90.1-2010 and later require engineers to calculate and document the expected pressure drop through each pipe segment in the critical circuit at design conditions. Maximum allowable velocities and pressure drops vary based on system type, operating hours, and pipe size.

Glycol System Considerations

Glycol solutions have higher viscosity and lower heat capacity than water. Always apply appropriate correction factors for:

  • Pressure drop (1.2x to 1.5x for typical concentrations)
  • Heat transfer capacity (0.85x to 0.95x reduction)
  • Pump power requirements (1.25x to 1.5x increase)
